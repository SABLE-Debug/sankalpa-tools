SANKALPA · DISCOVERY CALL · 01

Dad. The real money's in construction.

A 15-minute interview to find the one expensive, repetitive thing AI can kill — and to make his company the first proof. Keep it open while you talk.

Before you dial — the mindset

  • You're interviewing, not chatting. Tell him that up front — it earns his serious attention and he'll give you real answers.
  • He doesn't use AI — so never ask "where would you use AI." Ask about his pains. You do the AI translation in your head. He just talks about his business, which he loves.
  • Talk 20%, listen 80%. The longer he talks about a pain, the bigger it is. Let silences sit. Don't pitch — extract.
  • Your dad backs solid, not fragile. He's a planner — he'll get on board with anything if there's a start-to-finish plan with the failure-points mitigated, and the more value it brings the faster he says yes. So: lead with the value, and when he pushes on risk, that's a buying signal — "good question, here's how I make sure it can't fail" beats any hype.
  • Goal of the call: (1) find the #1 pain, (2) get the rand value of killing it, (3) get him to say yes to seeing your plan — not a blind commit. The plan wins him; the call earns the right to bring it.
01

Open — set it as serious

Warm, but deliberate. You're his son AND a founder doing real work. Both.

Dad, I want to pick your brain properly for about 15 minutes — like I'm interviewing you, not just chatting. I've gotten genuinely good at building AI tools, and I think construction is where the real money is. You're the best person I know in it. Can I ask you some real questions?
02

Frame — plant the seed early

Tell him what you're hunting for + that you want HIS company first. Plants the design-partner idea before you even ask.

Here's what I'm doing: I want to find the single most painful, expensive, repetitive thing in construction — and build an AI that just does it, automatically, every time. And I want to build it with your company first, so it's proven before I take it to anyone else. So — let's find that thing.
03

The interview — find the pain

Eight questions. Take notes right here (they save automatically). Don't rush — let him go deep on whatever lights him up; that's the signal.

Q1 · The map
Walk me through a normal week — where does the most time and money go that isn't actual building?
Listen for admin, chasing information, people waiting on people, paperwork. The overhead is where AI eats.
Q2 · The hated task
What's the one task you wish you never had to do again? The pure-friction thing.
Listen for emotion. The thing he sighs about = the wedge. Emotion marks the real pain.
Q3 · The slow bleed
Forget the big disasters — where do you lose money slowly? Rework, delays, chasing info, mistakes that compound?
Listen for a recurring cost. Recurring pain = recurring revenue for you.
Q4 · Tenders / bids
How much time goes into preparing a tender vs the actual work? And when you lose one — what's usually the reason?
Probe — a won tender is worth millions; this is my lead guess for the product. If his eyes light up here, dig hard.
Q5 · The clone
If you could clone your best estimator / PM / admin and have a copy work 24/7 — what would you have them do all day?
Listen for high-value repetitive expert work. That's the exact shape AI replaces. This question quietly finds the product.
Q6 · Paperwork & compliance
How much do you spend — time and money — on compliance, reporting, and paperwork? OHS, B-BBEE, project reports, all of it?
Probe — compliance is a recurring legal obligation = sticky subscription. Candidate #2.
Q7 · Is it everyone? (the TAM test)
When you talk to other construction owners — what do they complain about most? Would they all recognise [the thing you just named]?
Listen for "everyone has this." If the pain is industry-wide, it's a business, not a favour to dad.
Q8 · What's it worth?
If something just did [his #1 pain] automatically — perfectly, every time — what would that be worth to you per month? And what does not having it cost you?
Listen for a number. If he'd pay R10–25k/mo without flinching, you've found a high-ticket business.
04

Show him the magic

Now — and only now — point AI at the pain HE just described. This is where he gets excited and you "rope him in." Use his own words back at him.

You know how everyone's talking about AI? Most people — including you, no offence — don't realise it can already do exactly what you just described. That thing — [his #1 pain] — I can build an AI that does it on its own, every single time, faster and cheaper than a person. That's not the future. I can start building it this week.
05

The ask — make him the first

The close. You're not asking for money — you're offering him a head start and asking for proof + intros.

Here's what I want — and I'm not asking you to bet on a vibe. Let me take what we just found and bring you back a full plan: start to finish, every way it could fail and exactly how I've mitigated each one, and the rand it saves or makes you. You only back it once it's obviously solid. We build it with your company as the first one — you get it first and free while we prove it, I get a Project-of-the-Year case study, and once it's working you introduce me. Fair?
06

After the call — the plan that wins him

The call finds the pain. THIS wins the yes. Your dad backs what's planned to the tee and de-risked — so within a day, bring him one page:

  • The outcome + the number — the pain gone, in his words, and the rand it saves or makes (his figure from Q8). Value first.
  • Start → finish — plain steps: what you build, in what order, what he gives you (data, an hour of his estimator), what he gets back, by when.
  • How it can't fail — the 3 ways it could go wrong, each with its mitigation. This section is the sell — it's "solid, not fragile" on paper.
  • His risk = zero — free while you prove it, his data stays his, no lock-in, kill-switch any time. Nothing to lose, the number to gain.

The verdict — fill this before you hang up

If you can't fill all five, ask one more question. This is what the whole call was for.
